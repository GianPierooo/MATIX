# Bloque 4 — PLACEHOLDER (contenido sintético, reemplazar cuando suba el material real)

Bloque 4 · Inglés · Nivel B2 (Upper-Intermediate)

Foco del bloque: hipótesis y arrepentimientos del pasado, contar lo que otros
dijeron sin sonar robótico, deducir con seguridad o con cautela sobre cosas que
ya pasaron, ampliar el vocabulario con phrasal verbs de uso real, y aprender a
escribir un opinion essay defendido con conectores claros.

Pre-requisito: haber visto en bloques 1–3 los condicionales 0/1/2, los modales
básicos (must/should/might en presente), reported speech simple, y conectores
de contraste (but, however, although) y de causa (because, since).

## 1. Mixed Conditionals y Third Conditional

### 1.1 Third Conditional — arrepentimiento sobre el pasado

Estructura: `If + past perfect, would have + past participle.`

Se usa para hablar de algo que NO pasó en el pasado y su consecuencia hipotética
(también en el pasado).

Ejemplos:

- If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam. (No estudié → no aprobé.)
- If she had told me the truth, I would have helped her. (No me dijo la verdad
  → no la ayudé.)
- I wouldn't have missed the train if I had left earlier. (Llegué tarde → perdí
  el tren.)

Forma negativa y de pregunta:

- If I hadn't been so tired, I would have gone to the party.
- What would you have done if you had been in my place?

Nota de uso real: en conversación se contrae mucho — `If I'd known, I'd have
called you.` Suena nativo; "I would have" completo se reserva para escritura o
énfasis.

### 1.2 Mixed Conditional — pasado que afecta el presente

Estructura: `If + past perfect, would + base verb.`

La condición está en el pasado, pero la consecuencia es en el presente.

Ejemplos:

- If I had learned French at school, I would speak it fluently now. (No aprendí
  → no lo hablo ahora.)
- If she hadn't taken that job, she wouldn't be living in Lima today. (Tomó el
  trabajo → vive en Lima hoy.)
- If we had bought the apartment last year, we would be paying half the rent now.

También existe el inverso (presente que afecta el pasado), menos frecuente:
`If + simple past, would have + past participle.` Ej.: `If I were more careful,
I wouldn't have lost my keys yesterday.`

### 1.3 Cuándo usar cada uno (regla rápida)

- Todo en el pasado → Third conditional.
- Causa en el pasado, efecto en el presente → Mixed.
- Causa y efecto en el presente/futuro → Second conditional (revisión).

### 1.4 Mini-ejercicio

Completa con la forma correcta:

1. If I __________ (know) you were sick, I __________ (visit) you yesterday.
2. She __________ (be) a doctor today if she __________ (finish) medical school.
3. If they __________ (leave) earlier, they __________ (not / be) stuck in
   traffic right now.

(Respuestas: 1. had known / would have visited · 2. would be / had finished ·
3. had left / wouldn't be.)

## 2. Reported Speech (en profundidad)

### 2.1 Cambios de tiempo verbal

Cuando contamos lo que alguien dijo, el verbo "viaja un paso atrás" en el
tiempo (backshift):

| Estilo directo                | Reported speech                       |
|-------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| "I am tired."                 | She said she was tired.               |
| "I work in Lima."             | He said he worked in Lima.            |
| "I'm working on a project."   | She told me she was working on a project. |
| "I have finished."            | He said he had finished.              |
| "I finished yesterday."       | He said he had finished the day before. |
| "I will call you."            | She said she would call me.           |
| "I can help."                 | He said he could help.                |
| "I must go."                  | She said she had to go.               |

### 2.2 Cambios de pronombres, tiempo y lugar

- `now` → `then`
- `today` → `that day`
- `yesterday` → `the day before` / `the previous day`
- `tomorrow` → `the next day` / `the following day`
- `here` → `there`
- `this` → `that`

### 2.3 Reported questions

Las preguntas pierden la inversión y el auxiliar (orden normal de oración):

- "Where do you live?" → She asked me where I lived.
- "Are you coming?" → He asked if I was coming.
- "Have you finished?" → She asked whether I had finished.

Para sí/no questions: `if` o `whether`.

### 2.4 Reporting verbs (más allá de "say" y "tell")

El inglés natural rara vez usa solo `said`. Usa verbos específicos:

- `apologize for + -ing`: She apologized for being late.
- `offer to + verb`: He offered to help me move.
- `refuse to + verb`: They refused to sign the contract.
- `suggest + -ing` o `suggest that`: She suggested going out for dinner.
- `warn (somebody) (not) to + verb`: He warned me not to trust them.
- `agree to + verb`: They agreed to meet at six.
- `complain about + -ing`: She complained about not being heard.

### 2.5 Mini-ejercicio

Pasa al estilo indirecto:

1. "I have lived here for ten years," she said.
2. "Will you come to the meeting tomorrow?" he asked me.
3. "Don't open the door," she warned the children.

(Respuestas: 1. She said she had lived there for ten years. · 2. He asked me if
I would come to the meeting the next day. · 3. She warned the children not to
open the door.)

## 3. Modales de deducción sobre el pasado

Para razonar sobre algo que YA PASÓ, usamos `modal + have + past participle`.

### 3.1 must have — seguridad

Convicción de que algo ocurrió, basada en evidencia.

- The grass is wet. It must have rained last night.
- She didn't answer. She must have been busy.
- They look tired. They must have worked all night.

### 3.2 might / may / could have — posibilidad

Algo pudo haber pasado, pero no estamos seguros.

- He might have forgotten the meeting. (Tal vez lo olvidó.)
- The package may have been delivered to the wrong address.
- She could have called, but she didn't.

Nota: `could have` también significa "tenía la capacidad pero no lo hizo":
`You could have told me you weren't coming!` (Reproche suave.)

### 3.3 can't have / couldn't have — seguridad de que NO ocurrió

Opuesto de `must have`.

- She can't have known about the surprise — she looked genuinely shocked.
- They couldn't have finished already; it's only been an hour.

### 3.4 should have / shouldn't have — arrepentimiento o crítica

- I should have called my parents on their anniversary. (Pero no lo hice.)
- You shouldn't have spent so much money on that.

### 3.5 Mini-ejercicio

Elige el modal correcto:

1. The lights are off. They (must / might / can't) have gone to bed.
2. I can't find my phone. I (must / might / shouldn't) have left it at the café.
3. She passed the exam without studying. She (must / can't / could) be very smart.

(Respuestas: 1. must · 2. might · 3. must — última en presente, ojo: aquí es
deducción del presente, no del pasado.)

## 4. Phrasal Verbs intermedio-avanzado

Diez phrasal verbs muy frecuentes en uso real (no listas para memorizar — entran
mejor en ejemplos cortos):

### 4.1 set up

Significa establecer, montar, organizar.

- They set up a new branch in Cusco last year.
- I need to set up a meeting with the team.
- She set up her own consulting business at 28.

### 4.2 look into

Investigar algo, analizarlo.

- The police are looking into the case.
- I'll look into it and get back to you tomorrow.

### 4.3 get over

Superar (una enfermedad, una decepción, una ruptura).

- It took me months to get over the flu.
- She still hasn't gotten over the breakup.

### 4.4 put off

Posponer.

- We had to put off the wedding because of the pandemic.
- Don't put off until tomorrow what you can do today.

### 4.5 work out

Tiene dos usos comunes:

- Resolver, encontrar la solución: I can't work out this math problem.
- Hacer ejercicio: She works out at the gym three times a week.
- También: salir bien, funcionar: Things worked out in the end.

### 4.6 come across

Encontrar algo de casualidad.

- I came across an old photo while cleaning the attic.
- She came across as very confident in the interview. (= dar la impresión de.)

### 4.7 turn down

Rechazar (una oferta, una propuesta).

- He turned down a job in London to stay close to family.
- They turned down our offer without explanation.

### 4.8 give up

Rendirse, abandonar algo.

- Don't give up — you're almost there!
- She gave up smoking five years ago.

### 4.9 figure out

Entender, resolver (parecido a work out 1).

- I can't figure out why the app keeps crashing.
- We finally figured out how to use the new printer.

### 4.10 bring up

Mencionar un tema o criar (a un hijo).

- He brought up the budget issue at the meeting.
- She was brought up by her grandparents.

### 4.11 Mini-ejercicio

Reemplaza el verbo entre paréntesis por un phrasal verb del bloque:

1. They decided to (postpone) the project until next quarter.
2. I (encountered by chance) an interesting article online.
3. She (rejected) the promotion because of the relocation.
4. We need to (investigate) what went wrong.

(Respuestas: 1. put off · 2. came across · 3. turned down · 4. look into.)

## 5. Writing: el Opinion Essay

### 5.1 Estructura clásica (4 párrafos, ~250–300 palabras)

1. **Introducción** — presenta el tema y deja clara tu postura (thesis statement).
2. **Argumento 1** — razón principal a favor de tu postura, con ejemplo concreto.
3. **Argumento 2 / contraargumento refutado** — otra razón o reconoce el otro
   lado y dilo por qué tu postura sigue en pie.
4. **Conclusión** — repite tu postura con otras palabras y cierra con una idea
   amplia (sin abrir un tema nuevo).

### 5.2 Thesis statement (lo más importante)

Una frase, al final del primer párrafo, que diga claramente qué piensas. Mal:
"This essay will discuss the topic of social media." Bien: "Although social
media has clear benefits, its overall impact on teenagers is negative."

### 5.3 Conectores útiles (por función)

**Para añadir:** in addition, furthermore, moreover, also, what's more.

**Para contrastar:** however, on the other hand, nevertheless, despite this,
although, whereas.

**Para dar ejemplo:** for instance, for example, such as, in particular, to
illustrate.

**Para causa-efecto:** therefore, as a result, consequently, this leads to, due
to, because of.

**Para concluir:** in conclusion, to sum up, all things considered, on balance.

### 5.4 Frases-andamio (úsalas hasta que te salgan solas)

- I firmly believe that…
- There is no doubt that…
- It is widely accepted that…
- One of the main reasons is that…
- A clear example of this is…
- On the other hand, some argue that…
- While it is true that X, the fact remains that Y.
- To sum up, although both sides have valid points, I am convinced that…

### 5.5 Errores típicos del nivel B2

- Repetir `I think` cuatro veces (variar con "I believe", "in my view", "in my
  opinion", "from my perspective").
- Pasar al primer person plural inadvertidamente ("we should…" cuando estás
  defendiendo TU postura).
- Conectores mecánicos ("Firstly… secondly… thirdly…") — riegan rigidez. Mejor
  variar.
- Conclusión que abre un tema nuevo ("Also, social media is bad for the
  environment.") — cierra, no abras.

### 5.6 Mini-ejercicio de escritura

Escribe un opinion essay de 4 párrafos (~250 palabras) sobre uno de estos
temas. Usa al menos un conector de cada función (añadir, contrastar, ejemplo,
causa-efecto, concluir).

- Should mobile phones be banned in schools?
- Is remote work better than working in an office?
- Are video games a waste of time or a valid form of entertainment?

## Resumen de cierre del bloque

Al terminar este bloque deberías poder:

- Hablar de arrepentimientos del pasado y de cómo el pasado afecta el presente
  (third y mixed conditionals).
- Contar lo que otros dijeron de forma natural, usando reporting verbs
  específicos en vez de solo "said".
- Deducir sobre cosas que pasaron (must / might / can't have + p.p.).
- Reconocer y usar al menos 10 phrasal verbs frecuentes en contextos reales.
- Estructurar un opinion essay con thesis statement clara y conectores
  variados.
